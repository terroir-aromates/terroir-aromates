Guarana

Energy support, cognitive performance.

Description

Originating from the Brazilian Amazon, guarana is a shrub whose seeds are prized for their stimulating properties. In this article, we invite you to discover the multiple health benefits of guarana.

We will explore the various ways in which guarana consumption can improve cognitive performance and provide energy support thanks to its unique properties.

Stay with us to explore all aspects of this iconic plant, traditionally used to combat fatigue.

Guarana originates from Brazil, specifically the Brazilian Amazon, an ecosystem boasting exceptional biodiversity. The plant derives its name from the Guarani tribe (Indigenous Americans).

Amazonian Indigenous peoples harvested the seeds, roasting or drying them in the sun. After removing the husks, they obtained a paste by peeling the seeds, which was kneaded and rolled into sticks intended to be stored above a fire for several months.

To consume guarana, it was enough to grate this stick over a container and make a drink. This practice was particularly valued during times of scarcity, as the drink was known to reduce hunger and provide energy.

Indigenous peoples were pioneers in discovering the exceptional properties of guarana. Their uses date back to pre-Columbian times, where this plant was revered not only for its energizing virtues but also for its aphrodisiac effects. Deeply rooted in their culture, it symbolized a gift from the earth, capable of revitalizing both body and mind.
