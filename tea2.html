Classico Maté

A maté with vegetal and mineral notes, with smoky nuances.

Description

Maté, also known as the "Jesuits' tea," is an infusion originating from South America. Its name comes from the Quechua word "mathi," referring to the hollowed-out and dried gourd that forms a calabash, traditionally used to consume it. It is made from a tree called yerba maté, belonging to the holly family. This tree, native to South America, is cultivated in four countries: Argentina, Brazil, Paraguay, and Uruguay. To grow, it requires a subtropical climate, humidity, and plenty of sunlight. Once harvested, the leaves are heated to stop oxidation, then chopped and stored for a few months to enhance their aromatic concentration. The leaves are then calibrated and blended to achieve the desired grade (with or without stems, large or small leaves, etc.).

Originally, it was a central part of the Guarani Indians' diet due to its benefits. Naturally energizing and beneficial thanks to the caffeine and minerals present in its leaves, maté is the ideal drink for sports recovery.
